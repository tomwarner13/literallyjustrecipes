## Ingredients
- 2 red bell peppers, whole
- 4 tbsp EVOO
- 1/4 lb toasted walnuts
- 3 cloves garlic
- 3 tbsp tomato paste
- 3/4 cup plain breadcrumbs
- 2 tbsp pomegranate molasses[^1]
- 1 tbsp dried Aleppo flakes
- 1 tsp sugar
- 1 tsp sumac
- 1/2 tsp salt
- Hot sauce, to taste
- Chopped fresh parsley, for garnish

## Instructions

1. Preheat oven to 425.
2. Brush the bell peppers lightly with EVOO, and place in a cast-iron skillet or other oven-safe pan. Roast in the oven for 30 minutes until they start to blacken, turning over halfway through.
3. Remove the peppers to a metal bowl. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap for a few minutes to trap the steam. When sufficiently cool, peel them, remove stems and most seeds, and roughly chop them into quarters.
4. Add pepper pieces, remaining EVOO, and all other ingredients except parsley to a food processor. Blend into a smooth paste - it should have roughly the consistency of a hummus.
5. Transfer to a serving bowl and garnish with parsley. Refigeration is recommended for storage, but try to serve around room temperature.
6. Serve with crackers, sliced veggies, or pita chips.

[^1]: Substitute with 1 tbsp each of lemon juice and honey.
