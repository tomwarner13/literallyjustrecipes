## Ingredients

- 2 cups AP flour
- 2 cups sugar
- 1 tsp baking soda
- 1/4 tsp salt
- 1 cup butter
- 1/3 cup unsweetened cocoa (I like King Arthur Triple blend but any cocoa is fine)
- 2 eggs
- 1/2 cup buttermilk
- 1 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
- about 1 tsp red food coloring (optional) [^1]
- Cream cheese icing

## Instructions

1. Preheat oven to 350. Cut a piece of parchment to line a 12x12 or 10x15 ("jelly roll pan") and spray a little Pam into the pan, to hold the paper.
2. Mix the flour, sugar, baking soda, and salt in a large bowl; set aside.
3. In a medium saucepan, combine the butter, cocoa, and 1 c water (Don't try it in the microwave.  Dry cocoa and liquids in a microwave do not do well). Heat the mixture in the pan over medium/ high heat, STIRRING all the time until it just comes to a boil. Remove from heat.
4. Add the cocoa mixture to the flour mixture; use an electric mixer on medium high to mix until well blended. Add the buttermilk, eggs, vanilla, and red food color. Beat for one minute (batter will be thin). Pour into the prepared pan.  Tap the pan with the batter in it, on the counter, to release any large bubbles, and then gently swish a knife through the batter to release a few more bubbles.
5. Bake about 25 minutes - you want them done but still moist.
6. COOL completely before icing with cream cheese frosting.  You can also dust them with powdered sugar or a mix of powdered sugar and a little cocoa.  I like to remove them from the pan first, and then ice them.  You can cut them before or after icing.  If you cut before, you can make fancy swirls of the cream cheese icing, which is pretty.  If you cut after, you can just spread the icing over and then cut. Either way, you can toss on a few sprinkles (red white and blue are pretty for the summer, red and green for Christmas, and so on). 

Makes about 30 brownies.

*(recipe cred: my mother Dorothy)*

[^1]: I think red coloring has a funny taste and I don't really like things too red.  I do not add much coloring.  Dark cocoa also tends to take over from the red color in any case. The brownies will naturally have sort of a reddish hue (like a devil's food cake) because of the chemical reaction of the acids in the buttermilk and the cocoa. I like to leave it at that.
